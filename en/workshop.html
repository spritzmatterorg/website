Phishing Laboratory

An interactive lab designed to best prepare employees in recognizing spam and phishing threats.

Laboratory Structure
  1. Theoretical Part (1 hour):
    1. Introduction to phishing: What it is, how it works, and the main techniques used by cybercriminals.
    2. Warning signs: Analysis of real cases to identify typical indicators of a phishing email (language, suspicious links, urgent requests).
    3. Best practices: Practical advice to avoid falling into traps and recommended actions in case of suspicion.
  2. Practical Part and Gamification (2 hours):
    1. Participants access an interactive platform designed to simulate real-life scenarios.
    2. Through a gamified mode, employees analyze and classify a series of emails as phishing or legitimate, earning points for each correct response.
    3. Realistic simulations with increasing difficulty levels, testing the skills learned.

Why choose it?
Phishing is today one of the main threats to corporate security, with potentially devastating business impact. Our lab provides your employees with the tools and skills necessary to recognize and respond correctly to phishing attempts, thus strengthening the first line of defense of your organization: the human factor.

  1. Practical approach combining theory and real simulations, allowing employees to develop skills to identify phishing attempts and increasing their awareness.
  2. Solid theoretical foundation combined with gamification to make phishing training an interactive experience, ensuring greater engagement and better learning.
  3. Structured workshop with realistic simulations of increasing complexity, enabling participants to test their skills in a safe and controlled environment.

Investing in strengthening your employees’ skills reduces risks and protects the company from threats, potential financial losses, and reputational damage.

Artificial Intelligence and Cybersecurity: Opportunities and Challenges for Businesses

A 4-hour seminar to explore the role of Artificial Intelligence in cybersecurity, analyzing risks and opportunities, and providing practical tools for its adoption in businesses.

Duration: 4 hours
Description:
Artificial Intelligence is today one of the most powerful and transformative tools available to businesses, but it also brings new security challenges. This 4-hour seminar is designed for managers, IT professionals, and decision-makers who want to deeply understand AI’s potential and the risks associated with its use. The course is structured in two complementary parts.
Part 1 (2h 30min) – The evolution of Artificial Intelligence and cybersecurity risks
    This first section traces the historical evolution of AI, from its origins to recent developments, to understand why it has become a key factor for competitiveness.
  1. Next, the focus will shift to the link between AI and cybersecurity:
    1. using AI to strengthen digital defenses,
    2. potential offensive applications,
    3. the phenomenon of Adversarial AI and its implications.

Part 2 (1h 30min) – How to integrate AI in a company
    This session focuses on practical and strategic aspects of AI adoption in business. Guidelines will be provided on how to start a transformation journey, with particular attention to mindset change in data management. The session will conclude with an interactive self-assessment exercise, where participants analyze the strengths and improvement areas of their organization in AI adoption.

Seminar Objectives:
  1. Understand the evolution and current role of AI.
  2. Analyze risks and opportunities in cybersecurity.
  3. Provide practical tools to initiate an AI journey in a company.
  4. Encourage practical and personalized reflection through guided exercises.
